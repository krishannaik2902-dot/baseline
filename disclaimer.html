1. Not medical advice

Everything on this site — the assessment, your score, your plan, the protocol cards, the articles, the calculators — is general educational information. It is not medical advice, a diagnosis, a treatment, a therapy, or a substitute for any of those. Baseline is a rules engine that maps your self-reported answers to publicly available research and well-known habit protocols. It has no idea what your bloodwork says, what medication you take beyond what you told it, or what your family history is.

2. No doctor–patient relationship

Using Baseline does not create a doctor–patient, therapist–client, or any other professional relationship. Nobody involved in making this site has examined you. The people whose research we cite have never heard of you, or of us.

3. Talk to a professional first

Before changing your diet, exercise, supplements, sleep medication, or anything else your body depends on, talk to a qualified healthcare professional — especially if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, take prescription medication, have a diagnosed condition, have a history of disordered eating, or experience chest pain, pressure, or unusual breathlessness during activity. Never disregard professional medical advice, or delay seeking it, because of something you read here.

4. Emergencies

If something feels urgent, it probably is. Call your local emergency number (911 in the US, 999 in the UK, 112 in the EU). If you are struggling with your mental health: 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), 116 123 (Samaritans, UK & Ireland), or findahelpline.com anywhere else.

5. Supplements

Where the plan mentions supplements, it mentions only widely studied, generally available ones, at commonly labelled amounts, with the evidence graded honestly. Statements about supplements have not been evaluated by the FDA or any equivalent regulator, and no supplement is intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Supplements can interact with prescription medication — a pharmacist can check yours quickly and usually for free. We sell no supplements and earn nothing from any product mentioned anywhere on this site.

6. Individual results vary

The research we cite reports averages across study populations. You are not an average; you are one data point with your own genetics, history, and circumstances. Nothing here is a promise that any habit will produce any particular result for you.
