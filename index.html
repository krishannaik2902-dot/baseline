§ 01How it works

Twenty-nine blunt questions. One honest number.

You answer the questions a sharp GP would ask if they had more than nine minutes. A rules engine weighs your answers by strength of evidence and scores eight domains — sleep and movement count double, because in the research, they do.

  1. 01

    Answer honestly. How you actually sleep, how often you get out of breath, who you could call at 2am. Four minutes, with “prefer not to say” always allowed.

  2. 02

    Get your baseline. A 0–100 snapshot of your current habits across eight domains — not a diagnosis, and deliberately not a “biological age”.

  3. 03

    Work the plan. Week one is five small changes, not a life overhaul. Retake in eight weeks and try to beat your number.

§ 02Where the advice comes from

Named sources. Graded evidence. No secret sauce.

Every recommendation shows the rule that fired, the strength of the evidence behind it, and where it comes from. The famous protocols — Blueprint, Huberman Lab — appear where they agree with the underlying research, and get flagged where they run ahead of it.
