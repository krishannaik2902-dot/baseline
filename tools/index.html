Caffeine cut-off

Caffeine has a half-life of about 5–6 hours, so an afternoon coffee is still working at bedtime. A common rule of thumb is to stop 8–10 hours before bed. Tell it your bedtime:

Daily protein target

Protein defends muscle as you age. A widely used active-and-older-adult range is around 1.6 grams per kilogram of bodyweight — higher than the old 0.8g/kg guideline, which was set to prevent deficiency, not to preserve muscle. This gives you a sensible range, not a rule.

Zone 2 heart-rate range

Zone 2 is the easy, conversational pace that builds your aerobic base — the pace most people skip because it feels too gentle. This uses the standard age estimate of maximum heart rate (about 220 minus your age) and takes 60–70% of it. It’s an estimate; the talk test (can you speak in full sentences?) is the real guide.

These are simple estimates for general education, not medical advice. Heart-rate formulas vary between people; if you have a heart condition, talk to a doctor before using target heart rates.
